Conservation of Energy: The Foundation

The animation is not pre-set. It's a live physics simulation governed by the Conservation of Energy.

The Principle:

We assume the cart starts at height hstart (with a tiny "push") and trades its Potential Energy (PE) for Kinetic Energy (KE).

$$PE_{initial} = PE_{current} + KE_{current}$$
$$mgh_{start} = mgh + \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

üöÄ Velocity (Speed): v(x)

By solving the energy equation for v (velocity), we get the master formula for the cart's speed at any point.

Master Equation:

$$v(x) = \sqrt{2g(h_{start} - f(x))}$$

The "Three Equations":

Because f(x) has three different formulas, this master equation results in three different, specific velocity equations:

$$v_1(x) = \sqrt{2g(h_{start} - f_1(x))}$$
$$v_2(x) = \sqrt{2g(h_{start} - f_2(x))}$$
$$v_3(x) = \sqrt{2g(h_{start} - f_3(x))}$$

üéÆ In the Simulation:

Our code calculates this value 60 times per second to find the cart's true speed. This is why it speeds up in the valleys and slows down on the hills - pure physics!

‚ö° G-Forces: Gs(x)

The "apparent weight" a rider feels. It's a combination of Earth's gravity (1 G) and the centripetal acceleration from the curve.

Master Equation:

$$Gs(x) = \frac{1 + [f'(x)]^2 + 2(h_{start} - f(x)) \cdot f''(x)}{(1 + [f'(x)]^2)^{3/2}}$$

What Riders Feel:

üèîÔ∏è Valley Bottom (x=10)

f''(x) is positive ‚Üí Gs > 1

"Pressed into your seat" feeling

üéà Hill Top (x=20)

f''(x) is negative ‚Üí Gs < 1

"Airtime" or weightless feeling

G-Force Scale:

  • 1 G: Normal Earth gravity
  • 2-3 G: Intense but safe (pressed into seat)
  • 0.5 G: Light, floating sensation
  • 0 G: Complete weightlessness (airtime)
