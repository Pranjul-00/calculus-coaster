Physics Model: Realistic Motion

How conservation of energy creates authentic roller coaster physics through mathematical modeling and real-time calculations.

Conservation of Energy: The Foundation

The animation is not pre-set. It is a live physics simulation governed by the Conservation of Energy.

The Principle:

We assume the cart starts at height $H_{total}$ (incorporating initial push) and trades its Potential Energy (PE) for Kinetic Energy (KE).

$$PE_{top} = PE_{current} + KE_{current}$$
$$mgH_{total} = mgh + \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Velocity & Vectors

By solving the energy equation for v (velocity), we get the scalar speed. We then use derivatives to find the vector components.

Master Velocity Equation:

$$v(x) = \sqrt{2g(H_{total} - f(x))}$$

Vector Decomposition:

To move the cart on the screen, we decompose this scalar speed using the slope $f'(x)$ from our Theory section:

$$v_x = \frac{v}{\sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2}}$$
$$v_y = v_x \cdot f'(x)$$

G-Forces

The "apparent weight" a rider feels is the Normal Force. This is a combination of gravity and centripetal force.

Normal Force (G-Force) Equation:

This equation accounts for gravity's component perpendicular to the track and the centripetal acceleration required to turn the cart.

$$n = \frac{1 + [f'(x)]^2 + 2(H_{total} - f(x))f''(x)}{(1 + [f'(x)]^2)^{3/2}}$$

If $n > 1$, you feel heavy. If $n < 1$, you feel light (airtime).

When the track ends at $x=35$, the cart becomes a projectile. Here are the analytic solutions for Range and Time.

1. Total Flight Time ($t_{flight}$)

Solving the quadratic equation $y(t) = 0$ for time:

$$t_{flight} = \frac{v_{y0} + \sqrt{v_{y0}^2 + 2gy_0}}{g}$$

2. Horizontal Range ($R$)

Distance traveled from the ramp's end:

$$R = v_{x0} \cdot t_{flight} = v_{x0} \left( \frac{v_{y0} + \sqrt{v_{y0}^2 + 2gy_0}}{g} \right)$$
