Physics Model: Realistic Motion

How conservation of energy creates authentic roller coaster physics through mathematical modeling and real-time calculations.

Conservation of Energy: The Foundation

The animation is not pre-set. It is a live physics simulation governed by the Conservation of Energy.

The Principle:

We assume the cart starts at height hstart (with a tiny "push") and trades its Potential Energy (PE) for Kinetic Energy (KE).

$$PE_{initial} = PE_{current} + KE_{current}$$
$$mgh_{start} = mgh + \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Calculated Velocity v(x)

By solving the energy equation for v (velocity), we get the master formula for the cart's speed at any point.

Master Equation:

$$v(x) = \sqrt{2g(h_{start} - f(x))}$$

Vector Decomposition:

To move the cart on the screen, we decompose this scalar speed into vector components using the track angle $\theta$ derived from the slope $f'(x)$:

$$v_x = v \cos(\theta) = \frac{v}{\sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2}}$$
$$v_y = v \sin(\theta) = v_x \cdot f'(x)$$

G-Forces and Centripetal Acceleration

The "apparent weight" a rider feels depends on gravity and the centripetal acceleration caused by the track's curvature.

Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$):

$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{\rho} = v^2 \cdot \kappa$$

This explains why high speed ($v$) combined with high curvature ($\kappa$) creates the strongest forces.

Total G-Force Equation:

$$Gs(x) = \frac{1 + [f'(x)]^2 + 2(h_{start} - f(x)) \cdot f''(x)}{(1 + [f'(x)]^2)^{3/2}}$$

Once the cart leaves the track, it becomes a projectile. We must mathematically derive the exact time of flight and landing distance.

1. Initial Launch Conditions

At the end of the track (launch point), we have:

  • Launch Height: $y_0 = f(35)$
  • Launch Velocity Components: $v_{x0}, v_{y0}$ (calculated from energy & slope)

2. Deriving Flight Time ($t_{flight}$)

The vertical motion is governed by gravity ($g$). We need to find the time $t$ when the cart hits the ground ($y=0$).

$$y(t) = y_0 + v_{y0}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2 = 0$$

This is a quadratic equation in the form $At^2 + Bt + C = 0$, where:

$$A = -\frac{1}{2}g, \quad B = v_{y0}, \quad C = y_0$$

Using the Quadratic Formula $t = \frac{-B \pm \sqrt{B^2 - 4AC}}{2A}$:

$$t = \frac{-v_{y0} \pm \sqrt{v_{y0}^2 - 4(-\frac{1}{2}g)(y_0)}}{2(-\frac{1}{2}g)}$$

Simplifying (and choosing the positive root for time forward):

$$t_{flight} = \frac{v_{y0} + \sqrt{v_{y0}^2 + 2gy_0}}{g}$$

This is the exact equation our simulation uses to determine the flight duration.

3. Deriving Range ($R$)

Since horizontal velocity ($v_{x0}$) is constant (ignoring air resistance), the range is simply velocity times the calculated flight time.

$$R = v_{x0} \cdot t_{flight} = v_{x0} \left( \frac{v_{y0} + \sqrt{v_{y0}^2 + 2gy_0}}{g} \right)$$

This calculates exactly how far the cart travels horizontally from the moment it leaves the track.
